The Mobile Robotics Laboratory is dedicated to research and education in autonomous mobile systems. The lab provides a flexible environment for testing algorithms and conducting experiments with various robotic platforms.

## Facilities

Our 200 mÂ² laboratory space includes:
- Indoor testing arena with configurable obstacles
- Motion capture system for precise tracking
- Multiple robot platforms for individual and multi-agent experiments
- Dedicated computing resources for simulation and data processing

## Research Activities

The laboratory supports various research projects including:

1. **Autonomous Navigation**: Development and testing of navigation algorithms in complex environments
2. **Multi-Robot Coordination**: Experiments with teams of robots performing collaborative tasks
3. **Human-Robot Interaction**: Studies on safe navigation in shared spaces with humans

## Student Projects

The lab is open to students for:
- Bachelor and Master thesis projects
- Course projects in Mobile Robotics
- Doctoral research
- Student robotics competitions

## Equipment Details

### TurtleBot3 Platforms

The TurtleBot3 is an affordable, programmable, ROS-based mobile robot for education and research. Our fleet includes:
- Burger model for basic navigation tasks
- Waffle Pi model with enhanced sensors

### Motion Capture System

The OptiTrack system provides millimeter-level position accuracy for:
- Ground truth data collection
- Algorithm validation
- Multi-robot coordination experiments
